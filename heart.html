What is the Heart? (Wikipedia)

The heart is a muscular organ that pumps blood through the circulatory system. In humans, it's about the size of a closed fist, located in the middle mediastinum.

Did you know? The human heart beats ~2.5 billion times in a lifetime!

Quick Statistics

Weight250–350 g
Dimensions12×8×6 cm
Daily beats~100,000
Daily blood pumped~7,570 liters

Heart Anatomy

Four Chambers

  • Right Atrium – receives deoxygenated blood
  • Right Ventricle – pumps to lungs
  • Left Atrium – receives oxygenated blood
  • Left Ventricle – pumps to body (thickest wall)

Valves

  • Tricuspid, Pulmonary, Mitral, Aortic

Layers

  • Endocardium, Myocardium, Epicardium, Pericardium

Blood Flow Path

Body → Right Atrium → Right Ventricle → Lungs → Left Atrium → Left Ventricle → Body

Cardiac Cycle & Conduction

Systole (contraction) / Diastole (relaxation)

Electrical System

  • SA node (pacemaker) → AV node → Bundle of His → Purkinje fibers
ECG: P wave, QRS complex, T wave

Cardiac Output = HR × SV (~5 L/min resting)

Common Disorders

  • Coronary Artery Disease – plaque buildup
  • Arrhythmia – irregular heartbeat
  • Heart Failure – reduced pumping
  • Valvular disease – stenosis/regurgitation
  • Myocardial Infarction – heart attack
Prevention: exercise, healthy diet, no smoking.
