Major Lobes

The brain is the command center of the human nervous system. It receives input from sensory organs, interprets it, and sends output to muscles. It is the most complex organ, containing approximately 86 billion neurons.[reference:6]

Primary Functions

  • Cognition, memory, and learning
  • Sensory processing and motor control
  • Regulation of vital functions (heart rate, breathing)
  • Emotion and behavior

Major Lobes of the Cerebrum

  • Frontal Lobe – Executive functions, planning, personality.[reference:7]
  • Parietal Lobe – Sensory integration, spatial awareness.[reference:8]
  • Temporal Lobe – Auditory processing, memory, emotion.
  • Occipital Lobe – Visual processing.

Key Structures

  • Cerebellum – Balance, coordination, fine motor control.
  • Brainstem – Connects brain to spinal cord; controls breathing, heart rate.
  • Limbic System – Emotion, memory (includes hippocampus, amygdala).

Neural Communication

Neurons communicate via electrical and chemical signals. Action potentials travel down axons, triggering neurotransmitter release at synapses.

Protection & Support

The brain is protected by the skull, meninges (dura mater, arachnoid mater, pia mater), and cerebrospinal fluid (CSF). The blood-brain barrier regulates substance entry.
